## Executive Summary

Agriculture employs **60-80% of Africa's workforce** and accounts for **23% of Sub-Saharan Africa's GDP**, yet the sector faces critical challenges: climate change, low productivity, market inefficiencies, and limited access to finance. AI-powered AgriTech startups have raised over **$1.8 billion in the last decade**, though African companies captured only **2% of the $29.6 billion** in global agritech funding in 2022.

### Key Market Metrics
- **African Agritech Funding (Decade):** $1.8 billion
- **African Share of Global Agritech:** 2% (2022)
- **West African Smart Farming Startups:** 72 (12 funded)
- **Primary Technologies:** Satellite imagery, IoT sensors, AI yield prediction, market linkage
- **Target Farmers:** 50M+ smallholder farmers across Africa

## Sector Trends & Technologies

### 1. Satellite Imagery + AI (Most Scalable)

**Technology**
- **Data Sources:** Sentinel-2 (10m resolution, free), Landsat (30m, free), Planet Labs (3m, paid)
- **AI Models:** Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for crop health, yield prediction
- **Applications:**
  - Crop health monitoring (NDVI, chlorophyll content)
  - Yield forecasting (pre-harvest estimates)
  - Pest/disease outbreak detection
  - Soil moisture estimation

**Advantages**
- **Scalability:** Can cover millions of hectares
- **Cost-Effective:** Free satellite data + cloud processing
- **No Hardware:** Farmers don't need sensors

**Limitations**
- **Resolution:** 10m insufficient for <1 hectare plots (most African smallholders)
- **Cloud Cover:** Sahel has clear skies (good), but coastal West Africa has frequent clouds
- **Latency:** Satellite revisit time 3-5 days (not real-time)

**Best Use Cases**
- **Regional Monitoring:** Government early warning systems for drought/famine
- **Commercial Farms:** 50+ hectare farms where 10m resolution acceptable
- **Yield Forecasting:** National-level agricultural planning

---

### 2. IoT Sensors + AI (Most Accurate)

**Technology**
- **Sensors:** Soil moisture, temperature, rainfall, pH, nutrient levels
- **Connectivity:** Cellular (3G/4G), LoRaWAN (long-range, low-power), satellite
- **AI Models:** Time-series forecasting, anomaly detection

**Advantages**
- **Real-Time Data:** Minute-by-minute updates
- **Precision:** Ground-truth measurements vs. satellite estimates
- **Actionable:** Immediate irrigation/fertilizer alerts

**Limitations**
- **Cost:** $50-200 per sensor station (prohibitive for smallholders)
- **Maintenance:** Sensors fail, require calibration
- **Connectivity:** Cellular coverage poor in rural West Africa
- **Power:** Solar panels needed (theft risk)

**Best Use Cases**
- **High-Value Crops:** Cocoa, cashew, irrigated vegetables
- **Demonstration Farms:** Government/NGO training centers
- **Cooperative Deployment:** 100+ farmers share sensor network

**TOLBI's Innovation**
- **Sub-$50 Sensors:** Cost reduction through local manufacturing
- **Cooperative Model:** Villages share sensor stations
- **Hybrid Data:** Combines satellite + IoT for accuracy + coverage

---

### 3. Mobile-Based AI Advisory (Most Accessible)

**Technology**
- **SMS/USSD:** Works on basic feature phones
- **Voice Messages:** Pre-recorded agronomic tips in local languages
- **AI Personalization:** Recommendations based on farmer's location, crop, planting date

**Advantages**
- **Universal Access:** 80%+ of African farmers have feature phones
- **Low Data Cost:** SMS costs <$0.01
- **Language Support:** Easy to translate to local languages

**Limitations**
- **Limited Data:** Farmers must manually input information
- **No Real-Time Sensing:** Relies on farmer reporting
- **Scalability:** Generic advice vs. personalized

**Best Use Cases**
- **Extension Services:** Replace in-person agricultural advisors (1 per 10,000 farmers)
- **Weather Alerts:** Drought warnings, frost alerts, planting window recommendations
- **Market Prices:** Real-time commodity prices (like AgroCenta)

---

### 4. Drone Imagery (Emerging)

**Technology**
- **Resolution:** 2-5cm (100x better than satellite)
- **Deployment:** On-demand flights for specific farms
- **AI Analysis:** Same models as satellite (crop health, yield estimation)

**Advantages**
- **Ultra-High Resolution:** Can see individual plants
- **Flexible Timing:** Fly anytime (no cloud/revisit constraints)
- **Rapid Deployment:** Cover 100 hectares in 1 hour

**Limitations**
- **Cost:** $500-2,000 per flight (prohibitive for smallholders)
- **Regulatory:** Many African countries restrict drone use
- **Expertise:** Requires trained pilots
- **Processing:** Computationally intensive

**Best Use Cases**
- **Commercial Farms:** 100+ hectare farms
- **Pest Outbreak Response:** Targeted surveillance when outbreak reported
- **Soil Mapping:** One-time high-resolution soil surveys

**Example:** Kitovu uses drones for high-value commercial farms, satellite for smallholders
