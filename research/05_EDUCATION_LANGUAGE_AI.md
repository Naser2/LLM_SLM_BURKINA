## Part 1: Education AI Solutions

### Executive Summary

Africa's education crisis is severe: **40-60% school enrollment** in many countries, **1 teacher per 50-100 students** in rural areas, and **massive language barriers** (instruction in colonial languages vs. mother tongues). AI EdTech funding surged from **$90M (2020) to $650M (2024)**, with focus on mobile-first, offline-capable, local-language platforms.

### Key Metrics
- **EdTech Market Growth:** $90M (2020) ‚Üí $650M (2024)
- **CAGR:** 48%+ (2020-2024)
- **Key Trend:** AI-driven localization + offline-first design

**Problem**

   - Burkina Faso national curriculum (MENA standards)

   - Niger national curriculum

   - Track student progress
   - Identify struggling students

**Implementation**
- **Technology Partner:** M-Shule (Kenya) or Eneza Education
- **Pilot:** 10,000 students per country (rural focus)
- **Timeline:** 12 months pilot, 24 months national rollout
- **Cost:** $500K-1M per country (3-year program)

**Expected Impact**
- Reach 500,000+ students in 3 years
- 15-25% test score improvement
- Reduce dropout rates by 10-20%
- Enable education for 2M+ IDP children

## Part 2: Language/NLP AI Solutions

### Executive Summary

Africa has **2,000+ languages**, yet **ALL are classified as low-resource**. Global LLMs perform 30-50% worse on African languages vs. English. The **Masakhane** grassroots movement (2,000+ volunteers) and commercial players like **Lelapa AI** and **Vambo AI** are building African language models through community-driven data collection and fine-tuning.

### Key Metrics
- **African Languages:** 2,000+ (30% of world's languages)
- **NLP Coverage:** <50 languages have any support
- **LLM Performance Gap:** 30-50% lower on African languages vs. English
- **Major Players:** Masakhane, Lelapa AI, Vambo AI, GhanaNLP, Lesan AI

  - Fine-tuning multilingual models (mBERT, XLM-R)
