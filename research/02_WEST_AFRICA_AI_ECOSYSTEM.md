## Executive Summary

West Africa's AI ecosystem is rapidly emerging, dominated by **Nigeria** (400+ active AI firms) and **Ghana** (strong accelerator presence), with growing activity in **Senegal** and **CÃ´te d'Ivoire**. The region faces unique challenges: **Francophone funding gap** (<20% of African VC despite 40% population), **energy infrastructure deficits**, and **linguistic diversity** (200+ languages across the region).

### Key Regional Metrics
- **Nigeria:** 400+ active AI firms, largest ecosystem in Africa
- **Ghana:** MEST accelerator hub, strong EdTech presence
- **Senegal:** Emerging Francophone leader, LAfricaMobile ($7M raise)
- **Burkina Faso/Mali/Niger:** Nascent ecosystems, infrastructure challenges
- **Total Regional Funding:** Estimated $200M+ (2020-2025)

## Regional Challenges & Barriers

### 1. Francophone Funding Gap
- **Problem:** Francophone Africa receives <20% of VC funding despite 40% of population
- **Impact:** Senegal, CÃ´te d'Ivoire, Burkina Faso, Mali severely underfunded
- **Language Barrier:** Most accelerators, resources, networking in English
- **Recent Progress:** LAfricaMobile $7M raise shows emerging interest
- **Solution Needed:** Francophone-focused VCs, French-language accelerators

### 2. Energy Infrastructure Crisis
- **Problem:** Unreliable electricity across region except Ghana
- **Nigeria:** Frequent grid failures, expensive diesel generators
- **Burkina Faso/Mali/Niger:** <30% grid access nationally
- **Impact on AI:** Cannot run 24/7 compute, training interrupted
- **Current Workaround:** Cloud-only training, edge deployment

### 3. Linguistic Diversity
- **Challenge:** 200+ languages across West Africa
- **NLP Complexity:**
  - Tonal languages (Yoruba, Igbo)
  - Dialectal variation (Hausa across Nigeria/Niger)
  - Code-switching (English-Pidgin, French-Wolof)
  - Low-resource status for all languages
- **Data Scarcity:** Minimal training data for most languages
- **Progress:** Masakhane, GhanaNLP, Lesan AI working on solutions

### 4. Internet Connectivity
- **Rural-Urban Divide:** <10% rural internet access in many countries
- **Cost:** Mobile data expensive (>$5/GB in some markets)
- **Infrastructure:** Limited fiber optic coverage
- **Impact:** AI applications must work offline or via SMS/USSD

### 5. Regulatory Fragmentation
- **Problem:** 16 ECOWAS countries with different regulations
- **Data Localization:** Conflicting requirements
- **Fintech/Health:** Requires licenses in each country
- **AES Complication:** Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger potentially exiting ECOWAS
- **Solution Needed:** Harmonized West African AI/data policies

## Key Sectors for AI Application in West Africa

### 1. Agriculture (Highest Priority)
- **Workforce:** 60-80% of West Africans work in agriculture
- **Challenges:** Climate change, drought, low yields, market access
- **AI Solutions:**
  - Crop yield prediction (TOLBI, Kitovu)
  - Precision agriculture (satellite imagery)
  - Market linkage (AgroCenta)
  - Autonomous weeding (Ghana robotics startups)
- **Target Farmers:** 50M+ smallholders across region

### 2. Healthcare
- **Doctor Shortage:** 1 doctor per 5,000-10,000 people
- **Infant Mortality:** 10x higher than developed countries
- **Counterfeit Drugs:** 30%+ of pharmaceuticals
- **AI Solutions:**
  - Telemedicine (Zencey, Radease)
  - Drug authentication (RxAll)
  - Diagnostic support (Ilara Health, Healthtracka)

### 3. Financial Inclusion
- **Unbanked:** 60-70% of population
- **Mobile Money:** High penetration (Ghana, Nigeria leading)
- **AI Solutions:**
  - Credit scoring for unbanked (Djamo)
  - Conversational banking (Kudi.ai)
  - Fraud detection

### 4. Education
- **School Enrollment:** 40-60% in many countries
- **Teacher Shortage:** 1 teacher per 50-100 students in rural areas
- **AI Solutions:**
  - Personalized learning (M-Shule SMS platform)
  - Language learning (SPiiKA)
  - Offline content delivery (Eneza, Teesas)

## Recommendations for Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger (AES Countries)

### Immediate Priorities (0-12 Months)
1. **Finalize National AI Strategies:** Complete Burkina Faso's 2026-2028 AI Action Plan; Mali and Niger should draft similar plans
2. **Establish Regional AI Partnership:** Leverage AES alliance for shared AI infrastructure, data, talent
3. **Language Data Collection:** Urgent investment in Moore, Bambara, Fulfulde datasets (partner with Masakhane)
4. **Energy Infrastructure:** Solar-powered edge AI deployments as interim solution
5. **Talent Development:** University AI/ML curriculum, partnerships with Nigerian/Ghanaian institutions

### Medium-Term Goals (12-36 Months)
1. **Regional AI Hub:** Establish shared compute facility (potentially in Ouagadougou, Bamako, or Niamey)
2. **Agricultural AI Deployment:** Pilot TOLBI-style climate adaptation tools for Sahel farmers
3. **Telemedicine Platforms:** Deploy WhatsApp-based AI health bots in French, Moore, Bambara, Hausa
4. **Attract Francophone VCs:** Leverage LAfricaMobile success to attract French, Senegalese investors

### Long-Term Vision (3-5 Years)
1. **AES Language Model:** Joint Burkina Faso-Mali-Niger LLM for Moore, Bambara, Fulfulde, Hausa
2. **Regional Data Center:** AES-owned facility to reduce cloud dependency
3. **AI Export Industry:** Train models for broader Francophone Africa, Sahel region
